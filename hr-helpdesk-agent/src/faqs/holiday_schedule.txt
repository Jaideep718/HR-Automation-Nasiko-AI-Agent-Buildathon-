Topic: Company Holidays and Office Closures
Category: Time Off / Scheduling

Q: What are the company holidays for 2026?
A: Company-observed holidays for 2026:
- January 1 (Thursday) – New Year's Day
- January 19 (Monday) – Martin Luther King Jr. Day
- February 16 (Monday) – Presidents' Day
- May 25 (Monday) – Memorial Day
- July 4 (Saturday) – Independence Day (observed July 3, Friday)
- September 7 (Monday) – Labor Day
- November 26 (Thursday) – Thanksgiving Day
- November 27 (Friday) – Day after Thanksgiving
- December 24 (Thursday) – Christmas Eve
- December 25 (Friday) – Christmas Day
- December 31 (Thursday) – New Year's Eve

Q: Do I get paid for holidays?
A: Yes, all full-time and part-time employees (working 20+ hours per week) receive paid time off for all company-observed holidays listed above.

Q: What if I need to work on a company holiday?
A: If your role requires you to work on a company holiday, you will receive holiday pay (1.5x your regular rate) plus an additional floating holiday to use at a later date. This must be pre-approved by your manager.

Q: Can I take additional religious or cultural holidays off?
A: Yes, employees may request up to 2 additional floating holidays per year for religious or cultural observances not covered by the standard holiday schedule. Submit requests to hr@company.com at least 2 weeks in advance.

Q: What is a floating holiday?
A: A floating holiday is a paid day off that you can take at any time of the year, subject to manager approval. Every employee receives 2 floating holidays per calendar year, in addition to the standard holiday schedule.
